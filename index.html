What is A Digital Footprint?

Often when one accesses the internet he or she will leave a trail behind. Every time someone posts on social media, likes a video on YouTube, likes a post on instagram, or retweets a tweet, he or she leaves a record of that interaction. Everything someone has ever said online can never truly be removed from the internet. Someone may be able to remove a post, but others may have saved or screen-shotted that post. Even a task as small as viewing a website leaves a trail since many websites log the IP-addresses of the devices that access their domains. All of these permanently saved interactions are a part of one's digital footprint. One's digital footprint also includes the things others have posted about someone online. For example, if a news station releases an article criticizing the actions of Donald Trump, this article becomes a part of Trump’s digital footprint. Therefore, a digital footprint is anything that exists online that is about you or anything that you have put online yourself. 

Why Your Digital Footprint is Important

A person's digital footprint basically represents his or her personality. You can learn so much about a person solely by looking at what he or she posts on social media, and what is posted about him or her by others. Therefore many colleges actually research their applicants social media accounts before making a decision on whether to admit the applicant or not. In addition, most companies actually research a potential employee's social media before offering that person a job. Therefore, a person's digital footprint can have a huge impact on his or her life both positively and negatively. If a person's social media depicts immaturity, criminal activity, paraphernalia, alcoholism, or other mischievous activities, then his or her digital footprint can greatly hurt his/her chances of being accepted into a good college, being hired for a well paying job, and much more. Celebrities have even been cancelled for things they have said on social media over ten years ago. On the other hand, if a person's social media depicts maturity, respectfulness, looks professional, and other desirable traits or activities, then his or her digital footprint can actually help the chances of him/her being admitted into a good college, or can help the chances of him/her getting a good job.

Examples of how a digital footprint have/can negatively affect someone:

  • Jimmy Fallon:
    In 2000, Jimmy Fallon did a "blackface" when impersonating Chris Rock on an episode of Saturday Night Live. Earlier this year (2020) the hashtag "#jimmyfallonisoverparty" was trending on twitter because of Fallons actions on that SNL episode.     In 2011, Kevin Hart tweeted out the following statement: “Yo if my son comes home & try’s 2 play with my daughters doll house I’m going 2 break it over his head & say n my voice ‘stop that’s gay,". In 2019, this homophobic tweet was discovered by people and it greatly hurt Hart's reputation. People were enraged by his offensive tweet. Kevin Hart even decided to step down as the host of the 2019 Academy Awards as a result of the resurfacing of this tweet. 

How You Can Maintain A Positive Digital Footprint

1
Do not tag people in posts... You might be maintaining a positive digital footprint, but that does not mean your friends do.
2
Ask yourself if you would want your friends, family, co-workers, or employer to see your post before you decide to post it.
3
If you are in doubt about whether or not you should post something, do not post it.
4
Do not overshare on social media.
5
Do not share your login credentials and keep your login information secure (You do not want anyone posting things on your social media accounts in your name).

6
Be respectful!
